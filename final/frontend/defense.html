What is Phishing?

Phishing is a fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, or credit card details by pretending to be a trustworthy entity. Typically, phishing occurs through email or fake websites designed to look like legitimate ones.

How to Recognize Phishing Emails:

  • Suspicious sender: The email may come from an address that looks similar to a legitimate one, but with slight variations.
  • Urgency or threat: Phishing emails often create a sense of urgency, like claiming your account is compromised or threatening legal action.
  • Unusual requests: Be cautious of emails asking you to click on a link, download attachments, or provide sensitive information.
  • Strange or incorrect language: Phishing emails often contain poor grammar, awkward phrasing, or spelling mistakes.
  • Links and attachments: Hover over links to check if the URL matches the legitimate site and avoid opening unexpected attachments.
